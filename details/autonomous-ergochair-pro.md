## Overview

The Autonomous ErgoChair Pro (previously called ErgoChair 2) is an ergonomic office chair designed for all-day comfort at a mid-range price point. It offers six adjustable features, a breathable mesh fabric backrest and headrest, and multiple color options.

## Features

- **6 Adjustable ergonomic features:**
  - Headrest with height and tilt adjustments via friction lock mechanism
  - Backrest tension control with crank for recline up to 20 degrees
  - Lumbar support cushion with vertical position and two tension settings
  - Armrests with height adjustment (push-button lock) and free-moving forward/back, side-to-side, and rotation
  - Seat base with locking slide for forward/back positioning and tilt adjustment
  - Seat height adjustment (18 to 20 inches range)
- **Mesh fabric backrest** for breathable support
- **Curved mesh headrest** — breathable and springy, suitable for headset wearers
- **Thick fabric-covered foam seat cushion**
- **Weight capacity:** Up to 350 pounds
- **Height accommodation:** Suitable for users up to 6'5" tall
- **Color options:** Multiple colors available beyond basic black
- **Wheels:** Caster wheels that roll and pivot smoothly

## Build and Assembly

- Materials: Metal chair stem and seat base; mostly molded plastic, synthetic fabric for the rest
- Ships in one box (~50 lbs) with six chair parts and assembly hardware/tools included
- Assembly time: Under 20 minutes (though part labeling and instructions could be clearer)

## Pricing

- **Retail price:** $499
- **Trial period:** 30-day return window
- **Warranty:** 2-year warranty covering defects in materials and workmanship (does not cover normal wear and tear)

## Pros

- Extensive adjustability for personalized comfort
- Breathable mesh backrest for airflow
- Affordable compared to premium ergonomic chairs like the Herman Miller x Logitech G Embody ($1,830) or Razer Fujin Pro ($999.99)
- Lightweight and easy to move

## Cons

- Extensive use of plastic may affect long-term durability
- Armrest tops move freely with no way to lock them in position
- Foam seat cushion does not breathe and can get warm during extended use
- Assembly instructions and part labeling are unclear
