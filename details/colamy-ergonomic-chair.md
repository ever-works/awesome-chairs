## Overview

The Colamy Ergonomic Chair is described as potentially the new king of chairs under $300. Despite sharing a brand with the Kirin chair, the Colamy Ergonomic takes the quality a step further and is considered the most well-built chair in this price range, competing with chairs up to $500.

## Features

- Advanced adjustment system with buttons and levers that are sleek, easy to use, and click into place to confirm adjustments
- Seat depth adjustment comparable to the Steelcase Gesture
- Extensive range of adjustments for full ergonomic customization
- Comfortable mesh backrest with curvature similar to the Eurotech Vera chair
- Fan-favorite mesh design with supportive curve
- Comfortable, easy-to-adjust headrest
- Nice arm pads, although arm positioning is slightly forward for some users
- Seat comfortable enough for long hours of use

## Build Quality

- Described as the most well-built chair tested in the under-$300 range
- Premium construction quality rivaling chairs costing up to $500

## Adjustability

- Seat depth adjustment (depth slider)
- Height adjustment
- Advanced button-and-lever system with tactile click confirmation
- Fully adjustable headrest
- Wide range of arm adjustments

## Comfort

- Comfortable headrest with easy adjustment
- Mesh backrest with supportive curvature similar to the Vera chair
- Seat adequate for extended use, though not as plush as higher-end chairs
