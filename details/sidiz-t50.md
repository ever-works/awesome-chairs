## Overview

The SIDIZ T50 is a less expensive office chair from the SIDIZ brand. While it looks polished from a distance, closer inspection reveals flimsy build quality and cheap-feeling materials, particularly in the headrest.

## Headrest Assessment

- **Tier Rank:** D (unanimous across reviewers)
- **Adjustment:** Depth adjustment (forward tilt), but it will not hold the adjusted position
- **Comfort Aspects:**
  - Comfortable padding that allows contact without feeling the underlying frame
  - Good aesthetics — looks premium for the price point
  - The padding itself is decent when positioned correctly
- **Limitations:**
  - Extremely flimsy feel; the depth adjustment never actually holds its position, making the function essentially useless
  - Headrest is too small and has too much movement during use
  - Requires being perfectly centered in the chair to make contact with the headrest
  - Difficult to adjust — requires getting out of the chair to avoid pulling it all the way out
  - Feels light and cheap overall, with a build quality that does not match its appearance
- **Reviewer Consensus:** Unanimously rated D-tier. All three reviewers agreed the headrest is usable but awkward, and while it looks good, the flimsy construction and inability to hold adjustments make it a poor choice. Reviewers recommended purchasing the chair without the headrest

## Features

- Depth-adjustable headrest with forward tilt function (does not hold position)
- Visually appealing design that looks more expensive than it is
- Affordable price point compared to premium office chairs
- Comfortable padding when positioned correctly
