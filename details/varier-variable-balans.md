## Overview

The Variable Balans is the original kneeling chair, invented in 1979 by Hans Christian Mengshoel and Peter Opsvik. It remains the most recognizable kneeling chair in the world with high-end Scandinavian manufacturing quality.

## Features

- Beech plywood sled base that flexes for active movement
- Wide rocking range (larger than Thatsit Balans due to no backrest restriction)
- Good quality padding that does not bottom out during extended use
- Upholstery choices: polyester, wool, or leather in a wide range of colors
- Environmentally friendly materials
- Option to add a backrest separately

## Pros

- High-end build quality from Scandinavia
- Strong warranty
- Good knee/shin and seat comfort
- Wide range of motion for active sitting
- Good portability
- Environmentally friendly

## Cons

- Low sitting mobility (takes swiveling and wiggling to reposition)
- No adjustability (no seat height, no knee pad adjustments)
- May need optional backrest addition for longer sitting sessions

- Weight limit: Not specified (tested with average adult users)
- Seat height adjustment: None
- Range of motion: Wide rocking range via flexible wood frame
